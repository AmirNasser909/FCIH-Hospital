Cardiology

Heart disease or heart disease is a comprehensive term that refers to a variety of different heart diseases . In 2007, heart disease was the leading cause of death in the United States [1] [2], England , Canada and Wales , [3] and 25.4% of all deaths in the United States are due to these diseases.

Types of heart disease

Coronary heart disease

Coronary heart disease Coronary heart failure is meant to deliver an adequate amount of blood to the heart muscle and surrounding tissues. Individuals often confuse coronary heart disease with coronary artery disease , although the first may occur for other and different reasons, such as coronary angiogenesis.

Coronary artery disease is an atherosclerosis caused by the accumulation of fatty platelets or plaque (porridge) inside the walls of the arteries that feed the heart muscle. In addition, angina (chest pain) and myocardial infarction (heart attack) are symptoms of coronary heart disease and associated disease.

More than 459,000 Americans die each year from coronary heart disease [6] . In the UK , there are 101,000 deaths per year due to coronary heart disease.

Symptoms

The symptoms of heart disease depend on the type of heart disease that you have.

Symptoms of cardiovascular disease (atherosclerosis):

Symptoms can include:

  • Chest pain (angina).
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Pain, numbness, weakness or coldness in your legs or arms if the blood vessels in those parts of the body are tight.
  • Pain in the neck, jaw, throat, upper abdomen or back.

You may not be diagnosed with cardiovascular disease until you have a heart attack, angina, stroke or heart failure. It is important to watch cardiovascular symptoms and discuss concerns with your doctor. Cardiovascular disease can sometimes be found early through regular checkups.

Symptoms of heart disease caused by abnormal heartbeat (arrhythmia):

Irregular heartbeat is abnormal heartbeat. Your heart may beat very quickly, very slowly or irregularly. Symptoms of arrhythmia may include:

  • Flutter in your chest.
  • Heartbeat (arrhythmias).
  • Slow pulse (slow heart).
  • Chest pain or discomfort.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Rotor.
  • Dizziness.
  • Fainting.

Symptoms of heart disease caused by heart defects (congenital malformations):

Congenital heart defects are serious - defects that are born with the baby - usually become apparent immediately after birth.Symptoms of heart failure in children may include:

  • Pale gray or blue skin (bluish).
  • Swelling in the legs, abdomen or areas around the eyes.
  • In the infant, and shortness of breath during feeding, leading to a lack of weight increase the child well.

Congenital heart defects are less serious and are often not diagnosed until later in childhood or during puberty. Signs and symptoms of congenital heart defects that are usually not immediately life-threatening include:

  1. Feeling short of breath during exercise or activity.
  2. Easily tire during exercise or activity.
  3. Swelling in the hands, ankles or feet.

Symptoms of heart disease caused by impaired myocardium (myocardial infarction):

Myocardial infarction is the thickness and stiffness of myocardium. In the early stages of myocardial infarction, you may not have any symptoms. As the condition worsens, symptoms may include:

  1. Inability to breathe with effort or rest.
  2. Swelling in the legs, ankles and feet.
  3. fatigue.
  4. Irregular heartbeat that is fast, fluttering.
  5. Dizziness, dizziness and fainting.

Symptoms of heart disease caused by heart inflammation:

There are three types of heart infections:

  • Inflammation of the pericardium, which affects the surrounding tissues of the heart.
  • Myocarditis, which affects the middle layer muscles of the walls of the heart.
  • Endocarditis, which affects the internal membrane that separates the chambers and the heart valves.

Symptoms vary slightly with each type of infection, and symptoms of heart infection can include:

  1. Homa.
  2. Shortness of breath.
  3. Weakness or fatigue.
  4. Swelling in your legs or abdomen.
  5. Changes in the rhythm of your heart.
  6. Cough dry or persistent.
  7. Rash or unusual spots.

Symptoms of heart disease caused by heart valve diseases:

The heart contains four valves - aortic, coronary, pulmonary and triplex valves - valves may be damaged due to a variety of conditions that lead to narrowing, leakage, or improper closure. Symptoms depend on which valve does not work properly, and symptoms generally include:

  • fatigue.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Arrhythmia.
  • Swelling of the feet or ankles.
  • pain in chest.
  • Fainting.
